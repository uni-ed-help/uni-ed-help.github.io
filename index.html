Sarah Fida

Sarah, a senior at Leon M. Goldstein High School in Brooklyn, NY, has always been intrigued with the fields of computer science, aerospace engineering, biology, and chemistry. While tackling numerous college-level/advanced science and math courses, she realized that these fields are largely male dominated. Passionate about defying the gender gap in technology, Sarah participated in the Girls Who Code Summer Immersion Program 2019, where she forged relationships with like-minded young women. Wanting to learn more about computer science applications in a corporate setting, Goldman Sachs provided a unique environment for Sarah to pitch and complete projects in the languages of Python, HTML, CSS, Javascript, and C and to communicate with real analysts in the technology sector. Outside the world of computer science, Sarah takes pride in involving herself widely in her school's community and in numerous activities outside her school. As Vice-President of the Senior Council, President of the Neuroscience Club, and photographer for the school yearbook, Sarah learns valuable leadership skills. As part of the Science Olympiad team and competing in various regional competitions, she learned how to utilize her building skills to complete in engineering events. Not forgetting her passion for the arts, Sarah is the concertmaster of her community orchestra and knows 3 languages.

Camellia Guan

Camellia is a senior in the Academy for Medical Science and Technology at Bergen County Academies in Hackensack, NJ. She harbors a great interest in topics in computer science, engineering, and chemistry, including web development, programming, and organic chemistry. During her years in high school, she has learned Java, Python, HTML/CSS, and assembly language. She was the co-president of her school's Girls Who Code club and participated in the 2019 Girls Who Code Summer Immersion Program at Goldman Sachs, where she learned Arduino C and Javascript. Camellia has partaken in many in various CTFS and cybersecurity competitions such as Girls Go Cyberstart and PicoCTF. Her interest are widespread, evidenced by her positions as President of the Science Olympiad Team, competing at the state level for the past three years. She will also be a Microbiology Team Intern at Reckitt Benckiser during the 2019-2020 school year. During her free time, Camellia can be found reading, writing, needle felting, listening to rock music, or constructing redstone contraptions in Minecraft. She is always looking to advance her understanding in programming and is passionate about battling the gender disparity in tech.
